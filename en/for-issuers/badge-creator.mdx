The TM Creator is where you customize the 3D trophies that will be used as recognition in your events.

<Tip>
TMs are **free on every plan**. Create as many as you want at no additional cost.
</Tip>

## 3D Editor

### Editor

The 3D editor lets you create trophies in just a few steps, with real-time preview:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload an image">
    Upload a 2D image in JPG, SVG, or PDF. The editor automatically converts the file into a 3D model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize the trophy">
    In the editor, you can adjust:
    - **Geometry** — extrusion depth and bevel
    - **Materials** — realistic (PBR), glass, or prism
    - **Textures** — over 15 presets, such as metal, leather, marble, and holographic
    - **Colors** — solid or gradient
    - **Animations** — rotation, bounce, and breathing
    - **Text** — with independent font and material
    - **Lighting** — HDR environments and bloom/neon
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish">
    Review the final result and publish the TM.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
The editor is self-service. You have direct control over the final result, without depending on a designer to create or adjust the trophy.
</Info>

## Best practices

For best results in the 3D editor, follow these recommendations:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use monochromatic images">
    Prefer images in **black with no background**, or with a white background. Gradient backgrounds can interfere with the 3D conversion and produce unwanted artifacts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prioritize SVG or high-resolution images">
    The engine reads the image based on the file's detail level. Low-quality images can produce imperfections and unwanted shapes in the 3D object.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Separate elements for more control">
    For more detailed customization, separate strategic elements of the image. Each disconnected line or shape can become an **independent path**, allowing individual editing in the editor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Drafts

The editor automatically saves your work. Drafts remain available for **24 hours** and are listed on the editor page. Published projects go directly to the gallery.
