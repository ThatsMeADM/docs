## Native sharing options

That's Me offers native sharing of digital certificates to make it easy to promote your achievements with greater visibility and credibility. The feature was designed to be fast, simple, and compatible with all major digital channels.

## How it works

When you open any certificate or digital trophy, you'll see the **"Share Certificate"** button. When you click it, a window appears with:

- Clickable icons for the main platforms:
  - **Facebook**
  - **LinkedIn**
  - **X**
  - **WhatsApp**
  - **Email**
- A field with the **public, verifiable URL** of the achievement
- A button to quickly copy that link

This system lets you share the achievement easily, with an appropriate presentation on each platform.

## Link structure and metadata

Each certificate has a unique link in the format:

`https://app.thatsme.com.br/c/{id}`

This link is public, traceable, and requires no login to view, making external sharing seamless.

When this link is shared on social media, it can automatically generate a **rich preview** with metadata such as:

| **Metadata**   | **Content** |
| :------------- | :----------- |
| og:title       | Check out [User name]'s achievement |
| og:description | Recognition issued by [Organization name]. View the details and interact with the 3D trophy |
| og:image       | Trophy cover image |
| og:video       | Trophy highlight video |
| og:url         | Direct certificate link |
| og:type        | website |

<Info>
These metadata tags help display the content with more clarity and prominence on compatible platforms.
</Info>

## What the shared post looks like

When you share an achievement on social media, the post may display:

- Cover image or video of the TM
- User's name
- Event or achievement name
- Direct link to view the full certificate

<Check>
This structure is useful for anyone looking to promote an achievement with more context, reinforce credibility, or amplify the visibility of sports, educational, cultural, or corporate events.
</Check>

## Copy direct link

Beyond the native options, you can also copy the link manually and paste it wherever you like, such as newsletters, emails, landing pages, and websites.

<Note>
The link follows a public viewing and validation pattern and can be accessed by anyone.
</Note>
