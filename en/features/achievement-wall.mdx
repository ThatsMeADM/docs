The Achievement Wall is your profile on That's Me. It's where your achievements are gathered, automatically organized, and accessible at any time. You decide what to show and what to keep private.

## What the profile displays

- **Avatar, name, and bio** — your identity on the platform
- **Counters** — number of achievements, followers, and profiles you follow
- **Achievement grid** — cards grouped by year
- **Edit button** — quick access to profile settings

Each achievement card shows the 3D trophy image, the title, the issuer name, the date, the number of likes, and comments.

<Info>
**Company profiles** have two tabs: **Achievements** (issued certificates) and **Issuances** (public events created by the company).
</Info>

## Search and sorting

Find achievements quickly by company name, achievement title, or category.

Sort options:
- Newest (default)
- Oldest
- Achievement name A–Z
- Achievement name Z–A

The list uses infinite scrolling: new achievements load automatically as you browse.

## Followers and connections

Click the **Followers** or **Following** counters to open the full list. In each list you can:

- See avatar, username, name, and profile type (Issuer or Member)
- **In the Following tab**: unfollow with one click
- **In the Followers tab**: remove followers from your profile
- **On other users' profiles**: follow back

## Privacy controls

That's Me offers privacy at two levels:

### Profile privacy

Set this option in **Settings > Privacy**:

| Mode | Who sees your achievements |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Public** | Anyone who visits your profile |
| **Private** | Only you and your followers |

When someone visits a private profile without following you, they see only the avatar and a button to request to follow.

<Note>
Company profiles are always public. The private profile option is available only for members.
</Note>

### Per-achievement privacy

Each achievement can be individually set to **public** or **private**, regardless of the overall profile setting. Use the `...` menu on the card to change visibility.

This allows you to, for example, keep your profile public while hiding only specific achievements.

<Note>
A private achievement can still be accessed by others through the direct link to the full certificate page, if you choose to share it.
</Note>

<Tip>
New achievements are public by default. Change visibility from within your profile to make them private.
</Tip>

## Access

Click the <Icon icon="circle-user" /> **Profile** icon in the sidebar on desktop or the bottom bar on mobile to access your wall at any time.

<Tip>
Use the wall as a living portfolio: highlight your most relevant achievements, hide what's personal, and share your profile as an interactive resume.
</Tip>
