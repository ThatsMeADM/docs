The Achievement Wall is your profile on That's Me. It's where all your achievements live — automatically organized and accessible at any time. You have full control over what to show and what to keep private.

## What the profile displays

- **Avatar, name, and bio** — your identity on the platform
- **Counters** — number of achievements, followers, and following
- **Achievement grid** — cards grouped by year, newest first
- **Edit button** — quick access to profile settings

Each achievement card shows: 3D trophy image, title, issuer name, date, likes, and comments.

<Info>
**Company profiles** have two tabs: **Achievements** (issued certificates) and **Emissions** (events created by the company).
</Info>

## Search and sorting

Find achievements quickly by searching for company name, achievement title, category, subcategory, or username.

Sort options:
- Newest (default)
- Oldest
- Name A–Z
- Name Z–A

Scrolling is infinite — new achievements load automatically as you browse.

## Followers and connections

Click the **Followers** or **Following** counters to open the full list. In each list you can:

- See avatar, username, name, and type (Issuer or Member)
- **In the Following tab**: unfollow with one click
- **In the Followers tab**: remove followers from your profile
- **On others' profiles**: follow back

## Privacy controls

That's Me offers privacy at two levels:

### Profile privacy

Set in **Settings > Privacy**:

| Mode | Who sees your achievements |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Public** | Anyone who visits your profile |
| **Private** | Only you and your followers |

When someone visits a private profile without following, they see only the avatar and a message asking them to follow.

<Note>
Company profiles are always public. The private profile option is available only for individual accounts.
</Note>

### Per-achievement privacy

Each achievement can be individually set to **public** or **private**, regardless of the profile setting. Use the card menu to toggle visibility.

This allows you to, for example, keep your profile public but hide specific achievements you'd rather not display.

## Access

Click the <Icon icon="circle-user" /> **Profile** icon in the sidebar (desktop) or bottom bar (mobile) to access your wall at any time.

<Tip>
Use the wall as a living portfolio: highlight your most relevant achievements, hide what's personal, and share your profile as an interactive resume.
</Tip>
