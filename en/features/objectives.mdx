Objectives are how you tell the platform **what you want to achieve**. When you declare an objective, the recommendation engine considers not only what you've already done, but also where you want to go.

This changes the quality of recommendations: instead of only suggesting events related to your past, the platform also directs opportunities aligned with your intent.

<Info>
Objectives are optional. The platform works well without them, using only your achievement history. But with declared objectives, personalization becomes significantly more precise.
</Info>

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="You declare an objective">
    Choose a category and describe — in free text or with tags — what you want to achieve. It can be something broad like "become a better programmer" or specific like "complete the NYC Marathon".

    The platform accepts anything from a quick click (just the category) to a detailed description with tags, level, and a specific destination.
  </Step>

  <Step title="The platform understands your intent">
    If you write free text, the platform's intelligence automatically extracts **structured tags** from what you described. These tags connect your objective to the tag vocabulary that already exists in analyzed events.

    This solves the language problem: you write "run a marathon" and the system understands the relevant tags are `running`, `marathon`, `endurance`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Recommendations become more precise">
    Events that share tags with your objective get a boost in the recommendation ranking. The more tags in common, the larger the boost.

    The result: within the same category, events more aligned with your objective rise in the ranking while generic events drop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you can define

The objectives form is designed to work with minimal friction. Only the category is required — everything else is optional and can be expanded when needed.

### Main fields

| Field | Description | Required |
|-------|-------------|:--------:|
| **Category** | Sports, Education, Corporate, or Sociocultural | Yes |
| **Objective** | Free text describing what you want to achieve | No |
| **Tags of interest** | Structured tags that refine matching | No |

### Advanced fields (collapsed by default)

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Priority** | Low, Medium, or High — high priority objectives get extra weight |
| **Level** | Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, or Expert |
| **Strengths** | Skills you already have |
| **Format preference** | Online, in-person, city, or state |
| **Specific destination** | A concrete event or goal (e.g., TOEFL, NYC Marathon) |

<Tip>
When typing tags, the field offers suggestions based on tags already existing in the event directory. You can also create new tags by typing and pressing Enter.
</Tip>

## Tags of interest

Tags are the primary precision mechanism for objectives. They connect your intent directly to the event tag vocabulary.

When you create an objective with the tags `running` and `marathon`, events analyzed by the platform that have those same tags get priority in recommendations.

### How tags are generated

There are three ways:

1. **You choose manually** — in the autocomplete field, searching among registered tags
2. **The platform extracts from text** — if you write a free-text objective without adding tags, the intelligence extracts them automatically
3. **The platform detects from history** — auto-generated objectives (see below) come with tags based on your profile

## Matching events

When creating or editing an objective, the platform shows a preview of **events in the directory that match your tags**. This gives immediate visibility into what's available and confirms that the objective is well-defined.

The cards show the event name, image, and how many tags they share with your objective.

## Auto-generated objectives

The platform can detect patterns in your history and suggest objectives automatically.

When you accumulate **3 or more achievements** in the same category without having an active objective in it, the system creates a suggested objective with:

- The identified category
- The level inferred from your achievements
- Tags extracted from your history

Auto-generated objectives appear highlighted with a visual indicator and three options:

| Action | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| **Confirm** | The objective is incorporated into your profile |
| **Edit** | Opens the form for you to adjust before saving |
| **Discard** | Removes the suggestion |

<Note>
Auto-generated objectives do not affect your recommendations until confirmed or edited by you.
</Note>

## Auto-evolution

Your objectives evolve with you.

When the platform detects that your level in a category has advanced — based on achievements obtained — the corresponding objective's level is automatically updated.

**Example:** you create a Sports objective as a beginner. After completing running events assessed as intermediate and advanced, the objective is automatically promoted to reflect your new level.

When this happens, the objective displays an indicator that the level was auto-updated.

<Info>
Auto-evolution never reduces the level. It only promotes. And if you manually edit the level, your edit is respected.
</Info>

## Specific destinations

Beyond general direction objectives ("I want to improve at programming"), you can define **specific destinations**: a concrete goal you want to achieve.

Examples:
- NYC Marathon 42km
- TOEFL iBT
- AWS Solutions Architect Certification

When a destination is linked to an existing event in the directory, it receives maximum priority in recommendations. And even when the destination is external (with a URL), the engine looks for preparatory events that make sense as intermediate steps.

## How objectives impact recommendations

Objectives represent **35% of the weight** in the recommendation engine — the factor with the highest influence on ranking.

The impact works in layers:

| Signal | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Same category | Event rises in ranking |
| Tags in common | Event rises proportionally to the number of shared tags |
| Compatible level | Event gets an additional boost |
| High priority | The entire calculation is amplified |
| Exact destination | Event goes to the absolute top |

In practice, this means two events in the same category can have very different positions — the one with tags matching your objective appears above, and the one without drops below.

## The virtuous cycle

Objectives aren't static. They're part of a cycle that refines itself:

```
You declare an objective (with tags)
        │
        ▼
Recommendations become more precise
        │
        ▼
You earn relevant certificates
        │
        ▼
Your level in the category advances
        │
        ▼
The objective auto-evolves
        │
        ▼
Recommendations become even more precise
        │
        ▼
      (cycle)
```

Each achievement feeds the system. No reconfiguration needed — the simple act of participating and earning achievements progressively refines the quality of suggestions.

## Access

Access your objectives in **Settings > Objectives**, from the sidebar menu on desktop or the bottom bar on mobile.

<Tip>
Start with just the category and a short text. The system extracts the tags and does the rest. You can refine later, as your recommendations become more relevant.
</Tip>
