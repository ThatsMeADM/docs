## What is overage?

Overage lets you keep emitting certificates after your monthly quota is used up. Each extra issuance is charged per unit on your next invoice.

Overage is **active by default** on all paid plans. You can disable it or set a monthly spend limit at **Settings → Billing → Overage**.

## Pricing per issuance

| Plan | Without WhatsApp | With WhatsApp |
|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | R\$ 0.25 / \$0.05 | R\$ 0.40 / \$0.08 |
| **Pro** | Included in plan | R\$ 0.40 / \$0.08 |
| **Business** | Included in plan | R\$ 0.35 / \$0.07 |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Custom |

<Tip>
  On Pro and above, WhatsApp is included in all issuances — both within quota and overage.
  The "Without WhatsApp" column only applies to Starter.
</Tip>

## Setting a spend limit

In **Settings → Billing → Overage**, you can:

1. **Enable/disable** overage entirely
2. **Set a monthly cap** — e.g., R\$ 200/month

When your overage spend hits the cap:
- New issuances are **blocked** for the rest of the billing cycle
- You receive an **email alert** when you reach 80% and 100% of the cap
- Issuances resume when the billing cycle resets

## How the billing cycle works

Overage resets on your **billing cycle date** — the day you subscribed, not the 1st of the month.

All overage charges are added to your next Stripe invoice automatically.

## Practical example

> You're on the **Pro** plan (1,000 issuances/month).
> In August, you emitted **1,247** certificates.
> The 247 extra issuances cost R\$ 0.40 each = **R\$ 98.80** added to your September invoice.

## Disabling overage

If you disable overage in settings:
- Issuances stop when you hit your quota
- No extra charges are incurred
- You can re-enable overage at any time

<Warning>
  If overage is disabled and you hit your quota mid-event, remaining recipients won't receive certificates until the next billing cycle or until you upgrade your plan.
</Warning>
