## Certificate integrity

Every certificate has a SHA-256 hash generated at emission time.
Verify any certificate at:

```
https://thatsme.com.br/certificate/verify/[hash]
```

The hash is computed from: `post_id | event_id | badge_id | profile_id | user_id | created_at | kyb_verification_id`

This means:
- The hash is **immutable** — any tampering invalidates it
- Certificates from **verified issuers** include the KYB verification ID — proof the issuer was verified at emission time
- Even if an issuer loses verification later, previously issued certificates remain valid and auditable

## KYB — Know Your Business

Organizations can apply for verification in Settings → Verification.

Two paths to get verified:

| Path | Method | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Domain verification | Add a TXT record to your DNS | Immediate (BASIC status) |
| Document upload | Submit official registration document | 1–3 business days (VERIFIED status) |

**BASIC** status confirms domain control.
**VERIFIED** status appears as a checkmark on the issuer's profile and on every certificate they issue.
