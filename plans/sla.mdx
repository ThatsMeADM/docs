## What is SLA?

SLA (Service Level Agreement) is a formal guarantee that the platform will be available. In simple terms:

> It's a promise that That's Me will be up and running. On Enterprise, this promise is contractual — if we're down beyond the agreed limit, you get credits.

## Availability by plan

| Plan | Availability | Guarantee |
|---|---|---|
| Free | Best effort | No formal guarantee |
| Starter | Best effort | No formal guarantee |
| Pro | Best effort | No formal guarantee |
| Business | 99.5% target | Prioritized monitoring, no contractual obligation |
| Enterprise | **99.9% contractual** | ~8h max downtime/year, service credits if not met |

### What 99.9% means

99.9% availability means at most **8 hours and 46 minutes** of unplanned downtime per year. Scheduled maintenance windows are excluded.

If the Enterprise SLA is not met in a given month, you're entitled to service credits as defined in your contract.

## Support channels

| Plan | Channel | Response time |
|---|---|---|
| Free | Community + email | Best effort (up to 48h) |
| Starter | Email | 48h |
| Pro | Email | 24h |
| Business | Priority email | 24h |
| Enterprise | Dedicated channel | 4h |

### Enterprise support

Enterprise customers get:
- A **dedicated support channel** (Slack or Microsoft Teams)
- Response time of **4 hours** during business hours
- A named **customer success manager** for onboarding and ongoing support
- **Quarterly business reviews** to align on usage and roadmap

## Incident communication

All customers are notified of incidents through:
- [status.thatsme.com.br](https://status.thatsme.com.br) — real-time status page
- Email notifications for major incidents (if subscribed)

<Tip>
  Even on the Free plan, your certificates are always verifiable. The verification system is independent from the main platform and has its own redundancy.
</Tip>
